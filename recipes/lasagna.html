Lasagna

Description:

Lasagna is a traditional Italian dish made by layering wide, flat sheets of pasta with a variety of fillings. It typically includes a rich tomato-based meat sauce (like Bolognese), creamy béchamel sauce, and cheeses such as mozzarella, ricotta, or Parmesan. The dish is baked in the oven until golden and bubbly, resulting in a hearty, flavorful meal.

Ingredients:

  • Lasagna noodles
  • Ground beef or sausage
  • Tomato sauce
  • Tomato paste
  • Onion, finely chopped
  • Garlic, minced
  • Ricotta cheese
  • Shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Grated Parmesan cheese
  • Egg (optional, for ricotta mixture)
  • Italian seasoning
  • Salt and pepper
  • Olive oil

Steps:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook the lasagna noodles according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a skillet, heat olive oil and sauté the chopped onion and minced garlic until fragrant.
  4. Add ground beef or sausage to the skillet, cook until browned, and drain any excess fat.
  5. Stir in tomato sauce, tomato paste, Italian seasoning, salt, and pepper. Simmer for 10 minutes.
  6. In a bowl, mix ricotta cheese, grated Parmesan, shredded mozzarella, and an egg (if using).
  7. Spread a layer of meat sauce at the bottom of a baking dish.
  8. Place a layer of lasagna noodles over the sauce, followed by a layer of the cheese mixture.
  9. Repeat the layers (sauce, noodles, cheese) until all ingredients are used, finishing with a layer of sauce and mozzarella on top.
  10. Cover with aluminum foil and bake for 25 minutes.
  11. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 15 minutes until the cheese is golden and bubbly.
  12. Let the lasagna cool for 10 minutes before slicing and serving.
